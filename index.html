Scooby-Doo (2002)

Scooby-Doo (also known as Scooby-Doo: The Movie) is a 2002 American live-action/computer-animated fantasy adventure-horror comedy film based on the long-running Hanna-Barbera animated television franchise of the same name (Scooby Doo). This is the first installment in the Scooby-Doo live-action film series, some other movies are Scooby-Doo 2: Monsters Unleashed (2004), Scooby-Doo! The Mystery Begins (2009) and Scooby-Doo! Curse of the Lake Monster (2010). The plot in Scooby Doo (2002) revolves around Mystery Incorporated, which is the group of four young adults and a talking dog who solve mysteries, who reunite after a two-year disbandment to investigate a mystery at a popular horror-themed tropical island resort. Filming took place in and around Queensland, Australia and the film was released on June 17, 2002.
